Build a new "1099 vs W-2 Compare" calculator page for NetMoneyTools.

**File:** Create `src/pages/ContractorCompare.tsx` in the netmoneytools package

**What it should do:**
This is a side-by-side comparison tool showing someone the true financial difference between being a W-2 employee and a 1099 independent contractor for the SAME gross pay amount.

**Input fields:**
- Annual gross income ($)
- State
- Filing status (Single, Married Filing Jointly, etc.)
- Pre-tax deductions for W-2 (401k, health insurance, HSA)
- Business expenses for 1099 (deductible expenses)
- QBI deduction checkbox (20% qualified business income deduction — default checked for eligible 1099 work)

**Calculations to show side-by-side:**

| | W-2 Employee | 1099 Contractor |
|---|---|---|
| Gross Income | $X | $X |
| Pre-tax / Business deductions | $X | $X |
| Taxable Income | $X | $X |
| Federal Income Tax | $X | $X |
| State Income Tax | $X | $X |
| FICA (SS + Medicare) | 7.65% (employee half) | 15.3% Self-Employment Tax (on 92.35% of net) |
| Self-Employment Tax Deduction | N/A | Deduct 50% of SE tax from income |
| QBI Deduction (20%) | N/A | $X (if eligible) |
| **Total Tax** | $X | $X |
| **Take-Home Pay** | $X | $X |
| Employer-paid FICA (hidden cost) | 7.65% | N/A (contractor pays all) |
| **True Cost to Employer** | Gross + 7.65% FICA match | Just the gross payment |

**Key insight to display:**
- "To match W-2 take-home as a 1099 contractor, you need to charge: $X" (reverse calculation)
- "As a 1099 contractor, you pay $X more in taxes but can deduct $X in business expenses"
- "Your effective tax rate as W-2: X% | as 1099: Y%"

**Tax calculation logic:**
- Use existing federal and state tax tables from the project (check taxCalc.ts or paycheck calculator files)
- W-2 FICA: 6.2% Social Security (up to wage base) + 1.45% Medicare (no cap) = 7.65%
- 1099 Self-Employment Tax: 15.3% of 92.35% of net self-employment income
- 1099 gets to deduct 50% of SE tax from federal taxable income
- Add QBI: 20% deduction on qualified business income (after SE tax deduction, before federal tax)
